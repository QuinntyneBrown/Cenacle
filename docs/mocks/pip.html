Psalm 63 — a psalm of David, when he was in the wilderness

1O God, you are my God; earnestly I seek you; my soul thirsts for you, my flesh faints for you, as in a dry and weary land where there is no water.

2So I have looked upon you in the sanctuary, beholding your power and glory.

3Because your steadfast love is better than life, my lips will praise you.

Psalm 63:1–3 · ESV

You keep this passage open in another tab most mornings. It is the kind of reading that asks to be sat with — slowly, without the room going quiet around you.

Tonight the parish has gathered for Vespers. You did not want to close the psalm to be present, and you did not want to leave the prayer to keep reading. So you popped the gathering out. It floats now in the corner, above everything, small and steady, while the words stay in front of you.

Pastor Ade is leading. You can hear him, glance up, add your Amen — and look back down to the wilderness and the water without missing a breath.

The window in the corner stays on top of every app and tab. Close it and the gathering returns to its full room — nothing is lost, no one is dropped.
